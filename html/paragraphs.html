HTML Paragraphs

The <p> element encloses a paragraph.

    <p>A paragraph
    may consist of
    many lines.</p>

will appear as:

A paragraph may consist of many lines.

A browser will reformat text within a paragraph removing extra lines and spaces.

Most browsers will display paragraphs correctly if you forget the closing tag. Do not reply on this.

The <br> element inserts a line break. Use it when you want a new line without starting a new paragraph.

    <p>A paragraph <br /> with a line break.</p>

will appear as:

A paragraph
with a line break.

The <pre> element encloses pre-formatted text.

    <pre>
    Pre-formatted  text  with  extra
    lines  and  spaces.
    </pre>

will appear as: 

    Pre-formatted  text  with  extra
    lines  and  spaces.

A browser will display the text as is, with extra lines and spaces. It may also use a different (fixed width) font.
